Publisher Disclaimer

Important information for news publishers, content providers, and media organisations whose content may appear in ApliNews.

Last Updated: 1 June 2026
For Publishers: This disclaimer governs the relationship between Aplitiq Ltd and all third-party publishers, media organisations, and content providers whose content is displayed in the ApliNews application. If you are a publisher and have questions or concerns, please contact us.

1. Overview

ApliNews is a mobile news aggregation application developed and operated by Aplitiq Ltd ("Aplitiq", "we", "us"). The App collects, organises, and presents news content from a variety of third-party publishers and news organisations ("Publishers") to provide users with a personalised news reading experience.

This Publisher Disclaimer sets out the terms and limitations under which third-party content is accessed, displayed, and used within ApliNews. It also describes the standards Aplitiq expects of its publisher partners and the basis of our relationship with Publishers.

2. Content Responsibility

2.1 Publisher Responsibility

All news content, articles, images, videos, and other media displayed in ApliNews originate from and remain the sole responsibility of their respective Publishers. Aplitiq does not write, edit, produce, or alter the substance of any third-party content.

Each Publisher is entirely responsible for:

  • The accuracy, completeness, and truthfulness of all content they publish.
  • Compliance with applicable laws, including defamation law, copyright law, data protection regulations, and journalistic standards.
  • Obtaining all necessary rights, licences, and permissions for the content they produce and distribute.
  • Corrections, retractions, and clarifications to any published content.

2.2 Aplitiq's Role

Aplitiq acts solely as a technology platform and news aggregator. Our role is to collect publicly available news feeds and present them to users in an organised, AI-personalised manner. We do not exercise editorial control over the content of individual articles.

3. No Endorsement

The inclusion of any Publisher's content in ApliNews does not constitute an endorsement, recommendation, or affiliation by Aplitiq Ltd. We do not endorse the views, opinions, editorial positions, or claims made in any third-party article or publication.

Publisher names, logos, and trademarks displayed in ApliNews are the property of their respective owners and are used solely for the purpose of identifying the source of content. Such display does not imply any partnership, sponsorship, or endorsement relationship between Aplitiq and those organisations unless a formal agreement exists.

4. Publisher Standards

Aplitiq is committed to providing ApliNews users with access to credible, high-quality journalism. We therefore require that all Publishers whose content appears in the App meet the following minimum standards:

  • Editorial Integrity: Publishers must adhere to recognised journalistic standards, including clear separation of news reporting and opinion, transparent corrections policies, and named authorship where appropriate.
  • Accuracy: Publishers must make reasonable efforts to verify facts before publication and promptly correct material errors.
  • Transparency: Publishers must clearly disclose ownership, funding sources that may influence editorial output, and conflicts of interest where relevant.
  • Legal Compliance: Content must not violate applicable laws, including copyright, defamation, privacy, or hate speech legislation.
  • No Misinformation: Publishers must not deliberately publish false or misleading information. Content that consistently promotes demonstrably false narratives may result in removal from the platform.
  • Appropriate Content: Content must be appropriate for a general news audience. Publishers that consistently publish harmful, illegal, or explicit content will be removed from ApliNews.

Publishers who do not wish their content to appear in ApliNews may contact us to request removal. We will process such requests within a reasonable timeframe.

If you identify content within ApliNews that violates our Publisher Standards, applicable law, or contains dangerous misinformation, please report it to us via our Contact page. We will review all reports and take appropriate action, which may include removing a Publisher from the platform.
